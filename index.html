Monitor Progress

How does a board shift its agenda from operational reporting to genuine outcome monitoring?

The shift starts with the monitoring calendar, not the agenda template. Most boards try to redesign their meetings first — new agenda format, new consent agenda, new time limits. None of it sticks because the underlying question hasn't been answered: what student outcomes are we actually monitoring, and when?

Clarify Priorities

What's the difference between a board goal and a district goal?

Most boards confuse board goals (what students will achieve by when) with district goals (what the organization will do to get there). The distinction sounds academic until you try to hold anyone accountable for student outcomes.

Align Resources

Our budget doesn't connect to our goals. How do we fix that without a full strategic plan overhaul?

You don't need a new strategic plan. You need a budget narrative that asks one question for every major allocation: how does this spending connect to our student outcome goals? Start with the biggest budget lines and work backward.

How do we report student progress in a way that the community actually understands?

Most board progress reports are written for board members, not communities. The fix is simpler than most people think: one page, three numbers, one trend, one action. That's it.
